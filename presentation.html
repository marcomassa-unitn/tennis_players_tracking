HOUGH_THRESHOLD = 150 · MIN_LEN = 80
CLUSTER_RADIUS = 50 px

The doubles alley and net are rejected by matching detected line spacings to ITF court proportions; thin, washed-out far lines are recovered from a 1-D white-coverage profile and projective consistency.

Stage 1 · Geometry

Pixels → Metres

A single RANSAC homography from the 8 keypoints to the standard ITF court makes every later measurement metric — this one matrix underpins the whole project.

CourtConverter

H = findHomography(pix, ITF, RANSAC, 3px)
(x_m, y_m) = H · (x_px, y_px, 1)

Singles court: 8.23 m × 23.77 m
Service lines: 18 ft from each baseline

RANSAC tolerates a noisy keypoint; near-horizon points are guarded against ∞.

Why it matters

  • Player speeds in km/h, not pixels
  • Court zone occupancy in real bands
  • Shot positions on a true top-down map
  • Perspective-correct distances despite the camera angle
05
Stage 2 · Tracking · Earlier Attempts

Two Tracks That Didn't Hold

Before the current segmentation, two earlier approaches each broke in a way that left players unfollowed exactly when it mattered.

Attempt 1 — Mixture of Gaussians

  • Per-pixel MOG2 background model to carve out the moving foreground
  • Slow, content-dependent model: it kept relearning the players into the background when they paused or rallied in place
  • Camera micro-motion and broadcast compression flooded the mask with ghost blobs

Attempt 2 — Running average, hard warm-up

  • Running-average background (α = 0.5) + frame differencing
  • Detection was gated behind WARMUP_FRAMES = 30 — a fixed count, blind to frame rate
  • The opening ~1 s (first 30 frames) produced no boxes at all: the serve toss and first contact went untracked
if frame_idx >= WARMUP_FRAMES:  # nothing tracked before this → first 30 frames lost
→ fix: seconds-based warm-up + a static temporal-median reference so frame 0 is already tracked
06
Stage 2 · Tracking

Following Two Players

Players are the moving foreground. We segment them with a running-average background and keep their identities stable through crossings.

  • Running-average background (α = 0.15) + static warm-up reference
  • Frame difference → threshold (15) → morphology
  • Connected components, min area 300 px²
  • 2×2 minimum-cost association with a displacement gate (MAX_MOVE)
  • Identity-swap handling when players cross at the net
cost_A = d(p0,c0)+d(p1,c1)
cost_B = d(p0,c1)+d(p1,c0)
→ keep the cheaper pairing (IDs stable)

CSV: frame, player_id, x, y, w, h, cx, cy, area

07
Stage 3 · Analytics

Movement, Zones & Heatmaps

Projected to metres, each player's feet trajectory yields distance, speed and court coverage. Implausible jumps (> 45 km/h) are filtered as tracking glitches.

  • Per-player distance & mean / p95 / max speed (km/h)
  • 6 × 4 court zones — depth bands × lateral columns
  • Gaussian-smoothed heatmaps (P1 warm, P2 cool)
  • Animated top-down minimap of both players
08
Stage 4 · The Ball · Earlier Attempt

Why Classical Detection Failed

The first ball tracker used no model at all — only colour, motion and shape heuristics. On broadcast footage it simply could not lock onto the ball.

  • HSV yellow-green colour mask ∩ frame-difference motion
  • Contours filtered by area (5–150 px²), aspect ratio and circularity > 0.6
  • Motion blur stretches the fast ball into a streak — it stops being round, so the circularity gate rejects it
  • The same yellow-green appears on lines, logos and kit; the smallest blob picked was often not the ball
  • No model means no notion of "ball" — every frame is a fresh, context-free guess, so the track is empty or jumps
mask = motion & greenHSV
keep if 5 < area < 150
  & 0.5 < w/h < 2.0
  & circularity > 0.6
→ tiny + blurred + fast = no stable hit

Conclusion: hand-tuned thresholds can't generalise → switch to a learned detector

09
Stage 4 · The Ball

Tracking the Ball with YOLO

The ball is tiny, fast and often motion-blurred — the one place where a trained detector earns its keep.

  • YOLOv8 detector (ball_tracker.pt, conf 0.65)
  • Two-pass detection with frame-difference motion validation to reject static false positives
  • Interpolation fills short gaps; an interpolated flag marks them
detect → validate motion → interpolate

CSV: frame, cx, cy, w, h, area, interpolated

Stage 5 · Shots

When Was the Ball Hit?

A shot is a sudden change in the ball's vertical motion right next to a player. We read it from the smoothed ball track.

aSavitzky–Golay
smoothing
→
bvy sign reversal
or accel. peak
→
cplayer-proximity
gate
→
dmin-gap clustering
(keep strongest)
vy reversal ≥ 0.5 px/frame  OR
|acceleration| ≥ 1.5 px/frame²
AND ball inside expanded player box

Bounces also reverse the ball, but they happen away from the players — the proximity gate removes them. Clustered candidates collapse to one contact, keeping the strongest acceleration.

11
Stage 5 · Shots

What Kind of Shot?

Two orthogonal questions: which side of the body, and how the ball was struck — plus a conservative serve / smash detector.

AxisClassesCue
strokeforehand · backhandball vs body axis (+ near/far perspective)
shot_typeflat · slice · dropshot · lobscale-free pace, diefrac, reach, bowback
overheadserve · smashball above head + context (separate column)

Shot-type features are scale-free, so the camera angle (near player ~3× faster in pixels) does not bias them. Validated on a 23-shot ground truth: 21/23.

Delivery

One Command, Full Analysis

pipeline.py runs all seven stages end-to-end; live_view.py replays the result with synced overlays.

Unified pipeline

  • Headless by default, FPS auto-read from the video
  • Per-step isolation — one failure never aborts the run
  • Paths derived from the video name; --skip-* any stage
  • End summary: succeeded / skipped / failed

Live viewer

  • Video with player & ball boxes
  • Synced top-down minimap
  • Live shot statistics panel
  • End-of-clip summary: heatmap · zones · hitmap
